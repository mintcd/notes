Introduction

Principal tasks of proof theory (Buss 1998)

  1. to formulate systems of logic and sets of axioms which are appropriate for formalizing mathematical proofs and to characterize what results follow from certain axioms;

  2. to study the structure of formal proofs (normal forms, syntactic facts);

  3. to study additional information can be extracted from proofs beyond the truth of the theorem being proved (computational or constructive information);

  4. to study how best to construct formal proofs by a computer.
